Thermal Properties of Matter

Heat and temperature govern the behavior of matter. Bridges expand in summer, food cooks when thermal energy transfers, and water has a remarkably high specific heat.

Feynman Lens

Start with the simplest version: this lesson is about Thermal Properties of Matter. If you can explain the core idea to a friend using everyday language, examples, and one clear reason why it matters, you have moved from memorising to understanding.

Heat and temperature govern the behaviour of matter. A frozen nail is brittle; when heated, it becomes malleable. Bridges expand in summer and contract in winter. Food cooks when thermal energy transfers to it. This chapter explores how matter responds to temperature changes, how heat flows, and the physical foundations of thermal phenomena.
