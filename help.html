Install & sign in

ShikshaPal runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux laptops or desktop computers. The app does not run on phones or tablets — it needs a real computer because the AI runs locally on your machine.

  1. Download the installer Visit shikshapal.com and download the version for your operating system. The installer file is around 80–120 MB depending on the platform.
  2. Run the installer Open the downloaded file and follow the on-screen instructions. On Windows you may see a "Windows protected your PC" warning — click "More info" and then "Run anyway". On macOS, you may need to right-click the app and choose "Open" the first time.
  3. Create your account Open ShikshaPal. Sign up with the student's email address and a password. If the student is under 18, the app will also ask for a parent or guardian email and send a consent message there. The account is activated only after the parent confirms.
  4. Pick your class and language Choose the student's class (6 to 12) and preferred language of instruction. ShikshaPal will use this to recommend the right AI model and to set the right level of explanation.
  5. Download the AI model The app will recommend an AI model based on your computer's RAM and your class. This is a one-time download of 1–4 GB and may take a while on slower connections. Once downloaded, the model lives on your computer and works offline forever.
  6. Start asking questions Open a chapter, type a question, and the tutor will explain. Try things like "Explain Newton's third law with a real example" or "Help me factorise x² + 5x + 6 step by step".

System requirements

ShikshaPal runs entirely on your computer, so the device matters. Here are the minimum and recommended specifications:

Minimum (works, but slower)

  • Windows 10, macOS 11, or a recent Linux distribution;
  • 4 GB RAM (with at least 2 GB free when running);
  • 5 GB free disk space;
  • A 64-bit processor (any laptop made after 2017 should work);
  • An internet connection — only required for the first-time model download.

Recommended

  • 8 GB RAM or more, for smoother responses with larger models;
  • 10 GB free disk space if you want to keep multiple subject models installed;
  • SSD storage — model loading is much faster on SSDs than on old spinning hard drives.
Tip for parents

If your laptop is older or has only 4 GB of RAM, ShikshaPal will still work — it will just recommend the smaller, faster math-focused model. There is no need to upgrade your computer to use ShikshaPal.

Switching between models

If you ask the Math Tutor a chemistry question, it will politely tell you to switch to the Science Tutor. Click the model name at the top of the chat window to switch any time. You only download each model once — after that, switching is instant.

Asking better questions

The AI tutor works best when you treat it like a patient teacher rather than a search engine. Some tips:

  • Mention your class — "I'm in Class 9, can you explain..." gets answers at the right level.
  • Show your work — paste your attempted solution and ask "where did I go wrong?" instead of just asking for the answer.
  • Ask for examples — "give me three examples from daily life" makes abstract topics concrete.
  • Ask for a different explanation — if the first answer doesn't click, just say "explain it more simply" or "give me an analogy".
  • Keep your textbook handy — the tutor is a helper, not a replacement. Always cross-check against your NCERT textbook.
A note on honesty

The AI can be wrong, and sometimes very confidently wrong. Always check important answers against your textbook or your teacher — especially before tests. ShikshaPal is meant to help you understand topics, not to write your homework or exams for you.

Troubleshooting

The model download keeps failing or is very slow

Models are 1–4 GB, so the first download can take a while on slow connections. A few things to try:

  • Pause and resume — the app saves progress and continues from where it stopped.
  • Connect to a more stable network (home Wi-Fi tends to be more reliable than mobile hotspots).
  • Try a different time of day if your network is congested in the evenings.
  • If a download keeps failing midway, delete the partial file from the app's models folder and start again. The app will tell you the folder location under Settings → Storage.
The tutor is replying very slowly

Slow replies usually mean the model is too big for your computer. Open Settings → Model and switch to a smaller tutor — the Math Tutor is the lightest and runs well even on 4 GB laptops. Closing other heavy applications (browser tabs, video calls) before using ShikshaPal also helps.

I get an "out of memory" error

This means the model needs more RAM than your computer has free. Try:

  • Closing other applications, especially browsers with many tabs;
  • Switching to a smaller model in Settings → Model;
  • Restarting your computer to clear memory before opening ShikshaPal.
I forgot my password

On the sign-in screen, click "Forgot password?". We'll send a reset link to the registered email address. If the student is a minor, the link is sent to the parent's email so an adult is involved in the reset.

The parent consent email never arrived

Please check the spam or "Promotions" folder in the parent's inbox. The email comes from contact@shikshapal.com. If it is still missing after 30 minutes, write to us from the parent's email and we'll resend the consent link manually.

My antivirus is blocking ShikshaPal

Some antivirus tools are cautious about new applications, especially small-publisher apps. ShikshaPal is signed and safe — you can add it to your antivirus's allow list, or check our website for the published checksum to verify the installer file. If you are unsure, write to us.

The tutor gave me a wrong answer

It happens — AI is not perfect. Cross-check against your NCERT textbook or ask a teacher. You can also click the "Report" button below any reply to send the question and answer to us (no other personal data is included). Reports help us improve future versions.

Can I use ShikshaPal without signing in?

Not currently. We require sign-in so that we can provide parental consent for minor users, as required by Indian law. We are exploring a guest mode for older students in future versions.

How do I delete my account?

Open the app and go to Settings → Account → Delete Account, or write to contact@shikshapal.com from the registered email address. We delete accounts within 30 days of receiving a valid request.

For parents

If you are a parent or guardian, here are the most common questions:

  • What does ShikshaPal cost? Nothing. The app is free, with optional voluntary donations to support development.
  • Is my child's data safe? ShikshaPal collects only an email address, password (hashed), class, and language preference. The actual conversations stay on your device and are never uploaded. See the Privacy Policy for full details.
  • Are there ads? No. We will never show ads, and we will never sell or share your child's data.
  • Will it replace school? No, and that is by design. ShikshaPal points students back to their NCERT textbook, encourages them to consult teachers, and is meant only as a homework helper and explainer.
  • Can the AI go off-topic? The tutors are configured to stick to academic topics. If a student tries to chat about non-school things, the tutor will gently redirect them. No system is perfect, though, so we still recommend keeping the laptop in a shared family space.

When to ask a teacher instead

ShikshaPal is good for many things — explaining a concept differently, walking through a worked example, breaking down a definition. But it is not the right tool when:

  • You need help understanding your school's specific syllabus, exam pattern, or marking scheme;
  • You have a personal learning difficulty that needs an in-person diagnosis;
  • You need someone to motivate you, listen to you, or notice when you are struggling — that is what teachers and family are for;
  • You want feedback on a project, essay, or assignment that your school will grade;
  • You need medical, mental-health, legal, or financial advice — the AI is not qualified for these and should not be asked.
